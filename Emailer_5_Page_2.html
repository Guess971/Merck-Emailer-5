Sympathoinhibition refers to the reduction of sympathetic nervous
system (SNS) activity, which can lead to decreased heart rate (HR) and
blood pressure.
Beta-blockers and centrally
acting antihypertensive drugs
(e.g., clonidine, moxonidine) are
commonly used to achieve sym-
pathoinhibition.
Engaging in regular exercise,
practicing stress-reduction tech-
niques, and maintaining a healthy
diet can naturally reduce SNS ac-
tivity.
Lowering HR
decreases
myocardial
oxygen demand,
improving
cardiac efficiency.
Reduced SNS
activity leads to
vasodilation and
lower blood
pressure, aiding
in hypertension
management.
Sympathoinhibition
has been
associated with
reduced mortality
and morbidity in
cardiovascular
diseases.
